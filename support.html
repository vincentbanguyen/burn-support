FAQ

How does Burn work? Burn gives you a daily screen time allowance for selected apps. As you use those apps, your time depletes. Complete tasks to earn logs, then feed them to your flame to restore screen time. When you hit zero, your apps get blocked until you earn more.
What are logs and how do I use them? Logs are earned by completing tasks. Drag your collected logs into the flame to convert them into screen time — each log restores 10 minutes.
How do I select which apps to block? Use the app picker during onboarding or in Settings to choose the apps you want managed. You can update your selection at any time.
Why does Burn need Screen Time permission? Burn uses Apple's FamilyControls API to monitor app usage and manage blocking. This permission is required for the app to function. Your data stays on your device.
My apps aren't unblocking after feeding logs. Make sure you've dragged logs into the flame and your screen time balance is above zero. If the issue persists, try restarting the app or re-granting Screen Time permission in Settings.
When does my screen time reset? Your screen time balance resets daily at midnight. Unused time does not carry over to the next day.
Is my data private? Yes. All data is stored locally on your device. We collect nothing. See our Privacy Policy.
